# Reference

## **FAQ** (Frequently Asked Questions)

- *Why is the My Favorite Albums website not loading?*

There is likely an issue with your internet connection. Confirm that your device is connected to the internet and that the signal is strong. Turn off and reconnect your device to the internet, restart your browser, and then try to access the site again.

- *Whose data or website is this?*

There are no data or web app identifiers on the site. If someone sent you the link to this site, you might be able to recognize a common identifier, name, or email address in the URL. For example, the site [https://cholstro.shinyapps.io/shiny-music/](https://cholstro.shinyapps.io/shiny-music/) was published by the username “cholstro”, and [https://aidanbartlett0.shinyapps.io/shiny-music/](https://cholstro.shinyapps.io/shiny-music/) was published by the username “aidanbartlett0”. 

- *How do I publish My Favorite Albums with custom data?*

Please see the music enthusiast/developer documentation, including the conceptual overview, task topics, and reference. The task topics will walk you through the process of publishing the site step-by-step. 

## **Glossary**

* User Interface \- The graphical representation of the web app that a user can interact with in their browser. This is also sometimes referred to as a UI (user interface) or a GUI (graphical user interface).  
* Web app/application \- A public website that a user can interact with in a browser.   
* Shiny App \- The hosting and publishing method of choice for R-based web apps, such as My Favorite Albums.  
* R \- A coding language most commonly used in academia for data and statistical analysis.   
* Data \- A collection of information often stored as a table.  
* Backend \- The technical infrastructure that supports a website and allows it to function.  
* EP \- Extended play. An album featuring fewer songs than a typical album, but more than a single song.  
* Live album \- An album with songs that were recorded during a live event, show, or concert.  
* Vinyl \- An analog music storage medium that looks like a flat disc inscribed with ridges or grooves that convert vibrations into sound.
